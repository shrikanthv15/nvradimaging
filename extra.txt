Alexander Somwaru, M.D. is an Associate Professor at the Morsani College of Medicine at the
University of South Florida, Deputy Chief of Radiology for the James A. Haley Veteransâ€™ Affairs
Healthcare System, which is part of the largest integrated healthcare system in the country, and
Diplomate of the American Board of Radiology.
An expert in MRI and CT imaging, Dr. Somwaru is board-certified in diagnostic radiology and
subspecialty fellowship-trained in Body Imaging. He has particular clinical expertise in multi-
parametric prostate MRI and MRI-ultrasound fusion-guided prostate biopsies. He has an
established track record of translating innovations into standards of clinical care. His research
has resulted in over 30 exhibits at national and international conferences, 15 peer-reviewed
publications, several awards, and a book chapter. Dr. Somwaru is an associate editor and
reviewer for several peer-reviewed journals and regularly presents at society meetings such as
the Radiological Society of North America and Society of Abdominal Radiology.
Dr. Somwaru is a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania. After finishing medical school at
George Washington University, Dr. Somwaru completed his diagnostic radiology residency at the
University of Texas and a body imaging fellowship Johns Hopkins. He served as clinical faculty
at Georgetown University Hospital as an Assistant Professor and was unanimously voted the
Teacher of the Year. He was then recruited to Mount Sinai Hospital, where he rose to the rank of
Associate Professor at the Icahn School of Medicine and served as Quality Performance and
Improvement Lead.
Dr. Somwaru has translated a number of Artificial Intelligence (AI) clinical tools into practice,
like Clear Read AI and Rapid AI. He is a vocal advocate for Augmented, Virtual, and Mixed
Reality, which he has pioneered to enhance patient experiences, streamline biopsies, plan
reconstructive surgeries, and reduce operating room times for resect tumor resections.
Dr. Somwaru is active in the design and implementation of longitudinal curricula into diagnostic
radiology residency education. His educational mission is for radiologists to be completely
integrated into clinical practice through competency-based curricula and longitudinal
sequencing, which require engagement between trainees and educators, multidisciplinary and
team-based educational opportunities, and modality and systems-based practice opportunities for
preparation into real-world practice.
Dr. Somwaru has embraced partnership concepts into a transformational style of leadership that
he has learned on local, regional, and national levels to achieve improved communication, which
is integral to the complex environment of interdisciplinary care and team science.
